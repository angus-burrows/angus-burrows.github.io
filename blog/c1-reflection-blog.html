Reflection

What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

My thinking around the whole experience from where I see it as a beginner to this style of learning, Is that this not something you enter and then leave. The description and account from Shereef from Dev Boot Camp, was put simply; you only get out what you put in. And what he meant by this was you can only be a desired developer if you work hard and set your mind to it.

What are your impressions?

My impressions so far are that I am both excited and scared. Its exciting to be thrown into the unknown and learn to be sustainable while progressing towards a far more long term goal of being a professional web developer. Its scary to know that there is no way of guaranteeing full time employment, outside of being a discplined and self motivated individual.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I see myself being foremost honest and open, and with an open mindset I believe in myself and others to be capable of making the the experience not only a greater one, but our own. The ways in which this may develop I am uncertain, but I know that keeping focus on lifting everyone up together can only bring more help and achievement.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

Yes my expectations have shifted from the surface level, of how am I to obtain a new skill beneficial to my professional life to get me where I would like to be in the future, to how might I teach myself and others how to be more applicable in a workplace environment outside of Dev Academy.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

I am very excited to be a part of this work environment. I am not afraid to be a part of an environment that is ever changing and almost unflinching in the face of adversary, as well as becoming more and more progressive in regards to sustainability and ethics.
